The Three Basic Elements of Exposure (Aperture, shutter speed, ISO)

When shooting a photo with a mobile phone. You will not notice the aperture, shutter speed and, ISO. That does not mean they do not exist in the phone camera. You are just not allowed to adjust them individually on your phone because they are automatically set. In order to have greater latitude for creative expression, you have to learn the three elements.


Aperture

Aperture Value on the Lens


Aperture is the hole which controls the brightness passes through the lens. You can find the aperture ring on the lens. Aperture are usually written as “f/” followed by a number, such as f/1.4, f/2, f/2.8, f/4, f/5.6, f/8, f/11, f/16, f/22. Keep in mind, the smaller aperture, the larger f number. The larger aperture, the smaller f number. This can be confusing for beginners. For example, f/1.4 is a quite large aperture whereas f/22 is a quite small aperture. A larger aperture means that there are more light passes through the lens and falls on the image sensor than a smaller one.
Aperture also controls the depth of field. Depth of field is the distance between the nearest and farthest that are appears acceptably sharp. The smaller aperture, the larger depth of field. The larger aperture, the smaller depth of field. In a photo with large depth of filed, the objects that are out of focusing are clear compared to a photo with small depth of field. With small depth of field, the background will be blurred which can easily direct viewer’s attention toward the subject. But people do not always use small depth of field to shoot photo, if the background are indispensable for presenting the subject, shooting small depth of field will produce more desirable photo. There is no absolutely right or wrong. The photographers are free to choose the way they feel the best.

Shutter speed

Shutter speed is the length of time that the images sensor inside the camera is exposed to light. The typical shutter speeds are:

1/2s 1/125s
1/4s 1/250s
1/8s 1/500s
1/15s 1/1000s
1/30s

Fast shutter speed allows more light passes through than slow shutter speed. The exposure time 1/2s is the half of 1s. Fast shutter speed are used to capture fast moving object, such as animals, cars, and athletes. For example, to capture a clear image of a moving motorcycle, you probably need a shutter speed of 1/1000s. Otherwise, the motorcycle may appear blurred. You can rotate the dial on your camera to change the shutter speed.



ISO

It refers to the sensitivity of the camera’s sensor. Usually range from 160 to 25600. A high ISO will the image sensor to absorb more light. It will help you to shoot at a dark environment, but it will create noise at the same time. Noise will reduce the image quality. Just like a CD player, if you play it louder you definitely will hear more noise. The typical ISO values are:





50 1600
100 3200
200 6400
400 12800
800 25600
